How to read this page
A quick guide — no data background needed
👥 What are "groups" or "clusters"?
What does a group mean?
A group is a set of students who made the same types of mistakes on the quiz. The system automatically finds which students look most similar to each other — no labels or categories are set in advance.
Example: Group A = students who often mix up decimal placement. Group B = students who get the sign wrong.
How many groups should there be?
You choose the number of groups (K) before running the analysis. A good starting point is 3. More groups give more detail but can be harder to act on.
Example: K = 3 means "find 3 distinct groups of mistake patterns among my students."
❌ What are "errors" or "mistake types"?
What is a distractor category?
On a multiple-choice quiz, each wrong answer is linked to a type of mistake — for example, an off-by-one error, a sign error, or a rounding mistake. You define these labels yourself in the answer key before running the analysis.
Example: If a student picks choice B and B is labelled "sign error," that student recorded a sign error for that question.
What does "dominant mistake" mean?
The dominant mistake is the most common wrong-answer type across a group. If most students in a group picked wrong answers linked to decimal errors, the dominant mistake for that group is "decimal."
📊 How to read the Overall Evaluation
"How clear are the groups?" badge
Strong means students in different groups are very different from each other — clear separation. Moderate means most students fit well, but some are on the fence. Weak means the groups overlap a lot — use with caution.
"Are the groups consistent?" badge
This checks whether you get the same groups every time the analysis runs. High = very stable. Low = the groups shift around — treat them as approximate.
"Do groups differ by question?" badge
High means the groups answer individual questions differently — each group has a distinct weak spot. Low means the groups look similar question-by-question — a whole-class approach may work better.
🔬 What is in "Detailed Metrics"?
Do I need to look at the detailed metrics?
Not for everyday use. The Overall Evaluation at the top already summarises everything you need to make a teaching decision. The detailed section is there for anyone who wants to dig deeper — such as a researcher or data analyst reviewing the results.
